1. Who We Are

FairGame Initiative ("FairGame," "we," "us," or "our") is a nonprofit organization based in Ohio dedicated to expanding access to competitive science fair opportunities for K–12 students throughout the state. We operate the website at fairgameinitiative.org and the associated student, teacher, ambassador, and judge portals (collectively, the "Platform").

FairGame is organized as a nonprofit entity and is not subject to COPPA as a matter of federal law (COPPA applies to commercial websites; see 15 U.S.C. § 6502). However, we voluntarily adopt and exceed COPPA's protections because we believe the privacy of every student — especially those under 13 — must be actively safeguarded. Nothing in this policy should be interpreted to limit the protections described herein.

2. Who This Policy Applies To

This Privacy Policy applies to all individuals who interact with our Platform, including:

  • Students — K–12 students who register for or participate in FairGame programs (individual accounts)
  • Student Ambassadors — Student leaders who assist in program delivery and outreach
  • Teachers & School Professionals — Educators who register their students or participate in the program
  • Judges & Mentors — Adult professionals who volunteer through our platform
  • Visitors — Anyone who visits fairgameinitiative.org without creating an account

Different data practices apply to different user types. This policy describes each where relevant.

3. Children Under 13 — COPPA Notice

We take the privacy of children under 13 extremely seriously. Any student who indicates during registration that they are under 13 years of age will be subject to the following additional protections:

Age Confirmation Requirement

Every student registration form on our Platform includes an age confirmation checkbox that must be selected before account creation. Students who cannot truthfully confirm they are 13 or older will be directed to request that a parent or guardian assist with account setup.

Parental Consent for Under-13 Users

  • If a student indicates they are under 13, we will collect a parent or guardian email address and send a consent notification before that account is activated.
  • The account will remain inactive and no personal data from that student will be stored or used until a parent or guardian provides verifiable consent.
  • Parents may withdraw consent at any time by contacting us at fairgameinitiative@outlook.com. Upon withdrawal, the student's account will be deactivated and their data deleted within 30 days.

What We Collect from Under-13 Users

Even with parental consent, we limit data collection from students under 13 to the absolute minimum required to deliver program services: name, school, grade, project title, and project field. We do not collect any data beyond this from any student, regardless of age.

No Marketing to Children

We do not market any products or services to children. We do not use a child's personal information for any commercial purpose whatsoever. FairGame does not display advertising on its Platform.

5. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect only for the following purposes:

  • To operate and deliver the FairGame science fair program, including matching students with mentors and judges
  • To communicate with users about program events, deadlines, and updates
  • To populate the public judge directory (judges only; no student data is ever made public)
  • To display aggregated, non-personally-identifiable statistics on our homepage (e.g., total number of students served)
  • To monitor platform security and investigate unauthorized access
  • To respond to inquiries submitted through our contact forms
  • To comply with our legal obligations

We do not use your personal information for advertising, profiling, selling data, or any commercial purpose.

6. How We Share Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information. We share data only in the following limited circumstances:

Service Providers

We use Supabase (supabase.com) as our database and authentication provider. Supabase processes data on our behalf and is contractually bound to protect it. Supabase is SOC 2 Type II certified. No other third-party vendor receives access to student personal data.

Google OAuth

Users may optionally sign in via Google OAuth. If you choose this option, Google authenticates your identity and provides us with your email address and name. We do not receive any other Google account data. Google's privacy practices are governed by Google's own Privacy Policy.

Legal Requirements

We may disclose personal information if required by law, court order, or valid government request, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of FairGame, our users, or the public.

School Officials

Student information entered by or about students enrolled through a teacher account may be accessible to that student's teacher within the FairGame portal. This is consistent with FERPA's "school official" exception and constitutes a legitimate educational interest.

8. FERPA — Student Education Records

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), 20 U.S.C. § 1232g, protects the privacy of student education records at institutions that receive federal funding. FairGame works closely with schools and school districts and therefore treats all student information in our possession as subject to FERPA-equivalent protections, regardless of whether FERPA technically applies.

Teacher Responsibility

Teachers and school professionals who upload student information to FairGame's platform represent and warrant that they have appropriate school authorization and, where required by their school or district policy, parental consent to share such information with FairGame for program delivery purposes. FairGame's Terms of Service explicitly state this responsibility.

Minimum Necessary Data

We collect only the minimum information necessary to deliver program services. We never request information that is not directly required for program participation.

Parent and Student Rights

Parents of students under 18, and students 18 or older, have the right to:

  • Request access to and a copy of the student's data stored by FairGame
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of the student's data
  • Withdraw consent (for under-13 users) and have the account deactivated

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at fairgameinitiative@outlook.com. We will respond within 30 days.

9. How We Protect Your Data

We implement multiple layers of technical security to protect your information:

  • Row Level Security (RLS): Every table in our database has RLS enabled. Unauthenticated (anonymous) visitors can never read student records. Each authenticated user can only access data appropriate to their role.
  • Encrypted Storage: Uploaded files are stored in private, authenticated Supabase Storage buckets. Files cannot be accessed via a direct URL without an active authenticated session.
  • Signed URLs: File access is granted only through time-limited signed URLs that expire after one hour. These URLs are generated server-side and never exposed in browser URL parameters.
  • Password Security: Passwords are never stored in plaintext. All authentication is handled by Supabase Auth, which uses industry-standard bcrypt hashing.
  • HTTPS: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using TLS (HTTPS).
  • Access Control: Admin access is restricted to authorized FairGame personnel only. Role-based access is enforced through our portal system.

10. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to deliver the program and meet our legal obligations:

  • Active accounts: Data is retained while the account is active and the user remains enrolled in FairGame activities.
  • Inactive accounts: Accounts that have had no activity for 3 years may be deactivated and personal data removed, with notice sent to the registered email address prior to deletion.
  • Deleted accounts: When a user requests account deletion, personal identifying information will be removed within 30 days. Aggregated, non-identifiable records may be retained for program statistics.
  • Security logs: Event and access logs are retained for up to 2 years for security monitoring and incident response purposes.

11. Your Rights (Parents, Guardians & Students)

You have the following rights regarding your personal information or your child's personal information:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of the data we hold about you or your child.
  • Right to Correction: Request that inaccurate information be corrected.
  • Right to Deletion: Request that your account and associated data be deleted. We will process this within 30 days.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: For students under 13, a parent or guardian may withdraw consent at any time, which will result in account deactivation.
  • Right to Object: You may object to any processing of personal data that you believe is inconsistent with this policy.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at fairgameinitiative@outlook.com with the subject line "Privacy Request." We will verify your identity before processing any request and respond within 30 days.

12. Data Breach Procedures

In the event we become aware of unauthorized access to personal information stored on our Platform, we will:

  • Immediately revoke all active sessions and secure the affected systems
  • Investigate the nature and scope of the breach
  • Notify all affected users within 72 hours of discovery, describing what data may have been accessed and what steps we have taken
  • Notify relevant authorities as required by Ohio law and applicable federal regulations
  • Document the incident and retain records for at least two years

If you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately at fairgameinitiative@outlook.com.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify registered users via email. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Your continued use of the Platform after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy. If you do not agree to the updated terms, you should discontinue use of the Platform and contact us to request account deletion.
