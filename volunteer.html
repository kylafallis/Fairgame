Judging

Science Fair Judge

Evaluate student projects at a school, district, or state fair. Scoring rubric and training materials provided. No prior judging experience needed — just genuine STEM expertise.

3 to 5 hours on fair day
Evaluate 5 to 10 projects in your area of expertise
In-person or virtual options available
Counts toward corporate volunteer hour requirements
School, district, or state level openings
Mentoring

Student Mentor

Work one-on-one with a student throughout their science fair project. Help them develop their research question, experimental design, and analysis — without doing the work for them.

1 to 2 hours per month for 4 to 6 months
Virtual mentoring available, no geographic constraints
Matched based on your expertise and student interest
Mentor handbook and training provided
Optional: attend your mentee's fair as a spectator

Apply to volunteer

Judge Application

Fill out this form to join our judge network. We'll reach out when a fair near you needs judges in your expertise area.

Mentor Application

Apply to mentor a student through their science fair project. Mentorship runs from project proposal through competition day.

Applications are reviewed before matching. Because students are minors, all mentor applications are personally reviewed by the FairGame team before any student connection is made. If approved, you'll receive an invite link to our moderated Discord community.

Questions about volunteering? Email us at fairgameinitiative@outlook.com
