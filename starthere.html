Welcome to FairGame

Start your science fair journey.

Tell us who you are and we'll point you to exactly the right resources, steps, and support.

I want to do science fair
Whether your school has a program or not, we can get you from idea to competition.
1
Choose a research topic you genuinely care about - browse topic ideas in the Research Guide for inspiration.
2
Design your experiment - read the experimental design section to build a proper hypothesis, variables, and controls.
3
Register for your school or district fair - ask your teacher or reach out to us if your school doesn't have one yet.
4
Apply for a student ambassador mentor match - get paired with an experienced ISEF competitor for project feedback.
5
Learn the competition pathway - local fair to regional to state to ISEF. Know where you're going before you start.

I want to start or run a science fair
Everything you need to go from zero to a running program, with full support from our team.
1
Read the Complete Setup Guide - a month-by-month timeline from administrator buy-in to awards day.
2
Download the administrator proposal template - make the case to your principal or department head with data and a budget.
3
Register your fair and set up ProjectBoard - manage project submissions, judging, and scores digitally.
4
Recruit judges using our email templates - we'll help you find STEM professionals in your area.
5
Access the Teacher Portal for all downloadable rubrics, forms, permission slips, and checklists.

I want to judge or mentor students
Volunteer 3-5 hours as a judge, take on a mentee, or speak at a school event.
1
Complete the judge or mentor application at volunteer.html - takes about 5 minutes.
2
Select your area of expertise and availability - we match you to fairs and mentees that fit your schedule and background.
3
Receive your judge or mentor assignment - you'll get a confirmation email with all the details and logistics.
4
Review the judging rubric and evaluation criteria - available in the Teacher Portal and the volunteer confirmation email.

I want to support my child's project
Understand how science fairs work and how to help without doing the work for them.
1
Understand what makes a good science fair project - read the Research Guide overview together with your child.
2
Learn the competition pathway - school fair to district to state to ISEF. Understanding the ladder helps you plan ahead.
3
Know your role - ask good questions, help with logistics, and proofread. The research and conclusions must be your child's work.
4
Find local opportunities - use the State Resources page to see competition timelines and registration windows in your area.
